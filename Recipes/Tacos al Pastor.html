Tacos al Pastor

Corn tortilla filled with pork seasoned with a sauce of spices and dried chiles. The meat is skewered on a sword or shepherd that is turned manually while it cooks. Thin slices are cut from the browned meat, which is placed in the center of the tortilla; Served with small slices of roasted pineapple, chopped cilantro and onion. It is accompanied with chile de arbol sauce and, sometimes, lemon. Known in Monterrey as trompo tacos.

Preparation time

  • Total: Approximately 1 hour
  • Preparation: 30 minutes
  • Cooking: 30 minutesx

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the marinade. In a blender, place the guajillo and ancho chiles (previously deveined and seeded), the peeled garlic cloves, the onion chopped into large pieces, the oregano, cumin, black pepper, cinnamon and a pinch of salt. Add a little water and blend until you obtain a smooth and homogeneous sauce.
  2. Cut the pork into thin pieces and add to a large bowl. Pour the marinade over the meat and mix well to make sure each piece is coated with the sauce. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight, so the meat absorbs the flavors well.
  3. While the meat is marinating, prepare the pineapple. Peel the pineapple and cut it into thin slices.
  4. When the meat has finished marinating, heat a little oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the marinated meat and cook until well browned and cooked through, about 8-10 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, in another hot skillet, brown the pineapple slices for a few minutes on each side, until lightly caramelized.
  6. Heat the corn tortillas on a hot comal or skillet.
  7. To assemble the tacos, place some pork on each warm tortilla, then add some slices of caramelized pineapple. Sprinkle chopped cilantro and finely chopped white onion on top. Squeeze in some lemon juice and you're done!

Ingredients

  • 500 grams of pork (preferably leg or loin)
  • 1 pineapple
  • 1 large white onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 3 guajillo chiles
  • 2 ancho chiles
  • 1 tablespoon oregano
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Salt to taste
  • Corn tortillas
  • Chopped fresh cilantro
  • Finely chopped white onion
  • Lemons cut into wedges
